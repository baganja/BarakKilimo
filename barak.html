My Journey into GIS

My journey into geospatial engineering began in the remote villages of Kerio Valley, where I witnessed firsthand the profound challenges that geographic isolation and resource limitations impose on rural communities. Growing up in a banditry-prone region of Baringo County, I developed an early fascination with how geographic understanding could unlock solutions to the agricultural and developmental challenges that defined my childhood landscape.

As a young boy in Chepturu Primary School, I was captivated by the patterns of the land around me—the way seasonal rains transformed the valley, how elevation influenced crop yields, and how geographic factors determined which communities thrived or struggled. This curiosity evolved into a conviction that spatial analysis and remote sensing technologies could revolutionize how we approach agriculture, resource management, and community development in marginalized areas.

My academic journey took me from being the sole student from my primary school to advance to secondary education, through the competitive environment of Kamagut High School in Eldoret, and eventually to the prestigious St. Joseph's Boys School in Kitale. Each transition strengthened my resolve to pursue geospatial engineering as a means of creating meaningful change.

At Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology, I immersed myself in the intricate world of Geospatial Information Science and Remote Sensing. The theoretical foundations I gained there came alive during my practical experiences—from conducting land surveys in Kiambu County, where I honed my understanding of property boundaries and legal documentation, to implementing Plus Code Addressing systems in Kisumu County, where I witnessed technology's power to improve urban planning and service delivery.

Recognizing that the future of GIS lies at the intersection of traditional spatial analysis and modern web technologies, I expanded my expertise through specialized training at Zalego Academy in Westlands, focusing on programming and web development. This foundation led me to pursue advanced coursework in Web GIS development through Udemy and Harvard's CS50 Introduction to Programming with Python, integrating cutting-edge programming skills with my geospatial expertise.

My professional portfolio spans diverse sectors—from telecommunications infrastructure projects with Jamii Telecom to international web development for Terra Check, a geology company in Saudi Arabia. Each project has reinforced my belief that geospatial solutions can bridge the gap between complex geographic challenges and practical, scalable interventions.

Today, I specialize in creating sophisticated spatial analyses and cartographic solutions that support evidence-based decision-making across industries. My work is driven by the same passion that first emerged in the Kerio Valley—the conviction that understanding our geographic world is fundamental to creating equitable opportunities and sustainable development.

From the rural landscapes of Baringo to the technological corridors of Nairobi, my journey reflects a commitment to leveraging geospatial intelligence as a force for transformative change. I bring not just technical expertise in GIS platforms, remote sensing, and web development, but also the perspective of someone who understands how geographic solutions can reshape communities and lives.
